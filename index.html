Research

Optimal Design of Incentive Mechanisms: Badges and Their Role in Open Source Innovation

Thesis Paper

Abstract: I study how GitHub's badges (community recognition mechanism) change open-source output. Using a panel of about 49,000 users over one year, I find that badge earners create roughly 7% more new repositories per month after launch.

  • Use Poisson Quasi-MLE models with user and time effects to estimate badge effects on repositories, issues, and pull requests
  • Find a 5.1% drop in forking for badge users, which points to a shift toward original work
  • Outline simple changes to badge design that can raise engagement and innovation

Methods: Difference-in-Differences, Inverse Probability Weighting, Poisson panel models (~800,000 observations)

Playing to Win: Exploring the Causal Relationship Between Game Features and Positive User Feedback in the Steam Marketplace

Working Paper, 2024

Abstract: I analyze about 8,500 Steam games to see which features move user ratings. Achievements are linked to roughly 34% higher positive ratings. Early access games take a hit of about 40% in ratings.

Key Findings: Games with achievement systems tend to have more satisfied players. Early access releases face a clear rating penalty, which shows players care about polish and state of development. Playtime and price also matter for how users respond.

Methods: Weighted Least Squares, Heteroscedasticity Correction, Multiple Linear Regression

An Econometric Analysis of Uber Ride Behavior: A Case Study on NYC

Research Paper

Abstract: I measure how congestion surcharges affect shared rides in New York City. Using a cleaned sample of 50,000 trips drawn from 14 million rides, I find a 0.96% drop in shared ride usage after the surcharge.

Methods: Panel Data Regression, Fixed Effects Models, Policy Evaluation

Identifying Significant Factors Influencing Exonerations in the United States

Paper

Abstract: I use data from the National Registry of Exonerations (1989–2021) to track what drives wrongful convictions. Forensic errors and perjury stand out as the main contributors. After fixing multicollinearity, mean VIF drops from 31.81 to 1.88, which makes the patterns by race and gender much clearer.

Methods: Logistic Regression, Multicollinearity Diagnostics, VIF Analysis
