PROJECTS

Optimal Design of Incentive Mechanisms: Badges and Their Role in Open Source Innovation

  • Assessed GitHub’s Badges program on open-source innovation using a year-long panel dataset (~800k rows) of 49,000 users, employing inverse probability weighting and difference-in-differences methodology.
  • Identified a 7.3% monthly increase in new repository creation among badge earners post-launch.
  • Applied Poisson quasi-fixed-effects regression models to measure the role of specific badges on key outcomes such as repositories, issues, and pull requests.
  • Documented a 5.1% reduction in forking behavior among badge holders, reflecting a reallocation toward original contributions.
  • Proposed improvements to optimize incentives for badge design to enhance user engagement.

Playing to Win: Exploring the Causal Relationship Between Game Features and Positive User Feedback in the Steam Marketplace

  • Analyzed 8,500 video games using weighted least squares regression to address heteroscedasticity.
  • Determined that games with achievements experience 34% higher positive ratings on average.
  • Found early access games received 40% lower ratings, reflecting developmental feedback.
  • Revealed that playtime and price correlate with positive user ratings.
  • Offered insights for developers on achievements and release timing.

An Econometric Analysis on Uber Ride Behavior: A Case Study on NYC

  • Evaluated congestion surcharge impacts using a refined dataset of 50,000 records from 14M Uber rides.
  • Observed a 0.96% reduction in shared ride adoption due to surcharges.
  • Found temporal patterns, including increased shared ride usage on Thursdays and evenings.
  • Identified trip distance and duration as significant factors for ride adoption.

Identifying Significant Factors Influencing Exonerations in the United States

  • Examined exonerations from 1989 to 2021 using the National Registry of Exonerations.
  • Determined forensic errors and perjury as major contributors, with coefficients of 1.641 and 0.933.
  • Resolved multicollinearity, reducing the mean VIF from 31.81 to 1.88.
  • Highlighted systemic flaws, including racial and gender disparities in exoneration rates.
